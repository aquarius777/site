• What is ΣRGO?

    ΣRGO is a decentralized and open-source cryptocurrency that offers multiple improvements for different blockchain layers. The idea behind ΣRGO is to make cryptocurrencies decentralized again by providing user-friendly way for trustless blockchain exploring and verification. Ergo is more like Bitcoin and Ethereum, but it is updated version of the two for sure.

  • Why would businesses want to use Ergo over Stratis if they could achieve the same results(i.e. guaranteed verification of program execution properties like length, mem usage etc.) using C# as smart contract programming language solution(in Visual Studio)?

    For a general-purpose language, there's no guarantee of termination of a non-trivial program (it could be the case only for some special languages e.g. Coq or Agda)

    The answer may be quite complicated, but let's emphasize 2 points:

    1. It's hard (even impossible in theory) to estimate evaluation costs for programs on general-purpose languages like C# before evaluation itself. This leads to continuous DDoS attacks, like attacks to Ethereum last autumn. Sigma language cost analysis will be much easier.

    2. In Ethereum there is "gas limit", so you can't create really complicated contracts. And again, it's quite hard to estimate, whether some contract is possible in Ethereum (within gas limit) or not. Also this "gas limit" is not constant, so your contract may work now, but in future miners may reduce gas limit and you'll be out of business. So in theory everything is possible in Turing-complete languages, but in practice this leads to a lot of problems.
